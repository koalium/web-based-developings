Quantum Electrodynamics: Feynman Diagrams

Visualizing the first 5 QED processes with detailed calculations

1. Electron-Positron Annihilation

Process: e⁻ + e⁺ → γ + γ

Mathematical Description:

Step 1: Initial State
|i⟩ = |e⁻(p₁, s₁), e⁺(p₂, s₂)⟩

Where p₁, p₂ are 4-momenta and s₁, s₂ are spins.

Step 2: S-Matrix Element
S = -ie² ∫ d⁴x d⁴y ⟨γ(k₁, ε₁), γ(k₂, ε₂)|T[ψ̄(x)A̸(x)ψ(x) ψ̄(y)A̸(y)ψ(y)]|e⁻(p₁), e⁺(p₂)⟩

Using Wick's theorem, we get two possible contractions.

Step 3: Amplitude Calculation
M = -e² ε̸₁ (γ·(p₁ - k₁) - m)⁻¹ ε̸₂ + (1 ↔ 2)

Where the second term comes from exchanging the photons.

Step 4: Cross Section
dσ/dΩ = (α² / (2s)) * (1 + cos²θ)

Where α = e²/(4π) ≈ 1/137 is the fine structure constant, s = (p₁ + p₂)².

Step 5: Total Cross Section
σ = (π α² / s) * (1 - 4m²/s)^(1/2)

In the high-energy limit (s ≫ m²), σ ≈ π α² / s.

2. Compton Scattering

Process: γ + e⁻ → γ + e⁻

Mathematical Description:

Step 1: Kinematics
Initial: e⁻(p), γ(k) → Final: e⁻(p'), γ(k')

Energy-momentum conservation: p + k = p' + k'

Step 2: Amplitude
M = -ie² ε̸(k) [ (p̸ + k̸ + m)/((p+k)² - m²) ] ε̸'(k') + (k ↔ -k')

The second term comes from the crossed diagram.

Step 3: Klein-Nishina Formula
dσ/dΩ = (α² / (2m²)) * (ω'/ω)² * (ω/ω' + ω'/ω - sin²θ)

Where ω and ω' are initial and final photon energies.

Step 4: Energy Relation
ω' = ω / [1 + (ω/m)(1 - cosθ)]

This is the Compton wavelength shift formula.

Step 5: Limits

Low energy (ω ≪ m): dσ/dΩ ≈ (α²/m²) (1 + cos²θ)/2 (Thomson scattering)

High energy (ω ≫ m): dσ/dΩ ≈ (α²/(2mω)) (1 + cosθ)⁻¹

3. Electron-Electron Scattering (Møller Scattering)

Process: e⁻ + e⁻ → e⁻ + e⁻

Mathematical Description:

Step 1: Amplitude (t-channel)
M_t = -e² [ū(p₃)γᵘu(p₁)] [ū(p₄)γᵘu(p₂)] / t

Where t = (p₁ - p₃)² is the momentum transfer.

Step 2: Amplitude (u-channel)
M_u = +e² [ū(p₄)γᵘu(p₁)] [ū(p₃)γᵘu(p₂)] / u

Where u = (p₁ - p₄)², with a plus sign due to fermion exchange.

Step 3: Total Amplitude
M = M_t - M_u

The relative minus sign comes from Fermi-Dirac statistics.

Step 4: Differential Cross Section
dσ/dΩ = (α²/(8E²)) [ (1 + cos⁴(θ/2))/sin⁴(θ/2) + ... ]

In the center of mass frame, with E being electron energy.

Step 5: Non-relativistic Limit
dσ/dΩ = (α²/(16E²)) (1/sin⁴(θ/2) + 1/cos⁴(θ/2) - 1/(sin²(θ/2)cos²(θ/2)))

This is the Mott scattering formula for identical particles.

4. Electron-Positron Scattering (Bhabha Scattering)

Process: e⁻ + e⁺ → e⁻ + e⁺

Mathematical Description:

Step 1: Amplitude (t-channel)
M_t = -e² [ū(p₃)γᵘu(p₁)] [v̄(p₂)γᵘv(p₄)] / t

Where t = (p₁ - p₃)² is the momentum transfer.

Step 2: Amplitude (s-channel)
M_s = -e² [v̄(p₂)γᵘu(p₁)] [ū(p₃)γᵘv(p₄)] / s

Where s = (p₁ + p₂)² is the center of mass energy squared.

Step 3: Total Amplitude
M = M_t - M_s

The relative minus sign comes from the ordering of fermion operators.

Step 4: Differential Cross Section
dσ/dΩ = (α²/(8s)) [ (1 + cos⁴(θ/2))/sin⁴(θ/2) - (2cos⁴(θ/2))/sin²(θ/2) + (1+cos²θ)/2 ]

In the center of mass frame.

Step 5: High Energy Limit
dσ/dΩ ≈ (α²/s) (1 + cos²θ)/2

At high energies, the t-channel dominates for small angles.

5. Photon-Photon Scattering

Process: γ + γ → γ + γ

Mathematical Description:

Step 1: Effective Lagrangian
L_eff = (2α²/45m⁴) [(FᵘᵛFᵘᵛ)² + 7/4(FᵘᵛF̃ᵘᵛ)²]

This is the Euler-Heisenberg Lagrangian for low-energy photon-photon scattering.

Step 2: Amplitude
M = (α²ω⁴/(45π m⁴)) [ (ε₁·ε₂)(ε₃·ε₄) + (ε₁·ε₃)(ε₂·ε₄) + (ε₁·ε₄)(ε₂·ε₃) ]

For low-energy photons (ω ≪ m), where m is electron mass.

Step 3: Cross Section
σ = (973/10125π) (α⁴/m²) (ω/m)⁶

For unpolarized photons in the low-energy limit.

Step 4: High Energy Behavior
σ ≈ (α⁴/m²) f(ω/m)

Where f(x) approaches a constant as x → ∞.

Step 5: Physical Significance

This process demonstrates vacuum polarization and nonlinear effects in QED. It's a pure quantum effect with no classical analog.
