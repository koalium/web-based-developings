Quantum Chromodynamics (QCD) Fundamentals

Color Charge

Quarks carry one of three color charges: red, green, or blue. Antiquarks carry anticolor charges. The strong force mediates between color charges.

Gluons

Gluons are the force carriers of the strong interaction. They themselves carry color charge, leading to gluon self-interaction.

Confinement

Quarks are never observed in isolation due to color confinement. The energy required to separate quarks creates new quark-antiquark pairs.

Valence vs Sea Quarks

The proton contains three valence quarks (uud) that define its quantum numbers, plus a "sea" of virtual quark-antiquark pairs and gluons.

QCD Lagrangian

LQCD = -¼ Faμν Faμν + ∑f ψ̄f (iγμDμ - mf) ψf

Where Faμν is the gluon field strength tensor and Dμ is the covariant derivative containing the gluon fields.

Beta Function

β(g) = - (11 - ⅔ nf) g³/(16π²) + O(g⁵)

This describes how the strong coupling constant changes with energy scale, leading to asymptotic freedom at high energies.
